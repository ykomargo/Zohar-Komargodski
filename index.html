Zohar Komargodski

I'm primarily fascinated by Quantum Field Theory (QFT), a remarkably versatile and influential framework that has profoundly impacted various fields of physics and beyond. Although the term "quantum" in QFT might suggest a connection to quantum mechanics, its principles are also extensively applied in classical statistical mechanics, stochastic equations, the stock market, and more. I like to view QFT as akin to the calculus of physics—providing the right framework for any problem with a well-defined continuum limit. My interests lie both in the foundational aspects of QFT, such as general theorems and the development of its formalism, and in its concrete applications to physical systems. I am also intrigued by the connections between QFT and gravitation. Despite QFT’s remarkable success in delivering the most accurate predictions in physics history, it remains a subject of deep mystery for mathematicians. Since the summer of 2021, among other interests, I have been exploring the development of extended operators and defects (impurities) within QFT. With my collaborators, we've established powerful theorems regarding renormalization group flows on defects, examined various examples of magnetic and charged defects, and are currently delving into symmetry defects, crystal disclinations and dislocations etc.
